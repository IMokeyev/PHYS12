Project Concept:

A safe, accurate, stablized, remote control robotic arm that allows the user to do precise work from afar, whether it be from a few meters away due to dangerous materials, or from miles away to perform the work remotely.

This arm would use camera hand tracking technology from the MediaPipe library in order to capture the movements and position of the user's hand, then send it over to a microcontroller such as an ESP32, and translate it into motion of the arm, wrist, and phalanges through space.

Now in order to move each of the fingers precisely, I would use a system of aritificial tendons, which could be tightened and loosesen through motor control, much like the diagram on the left.

The parts would likely be 3D printed for cheap and accurate manufacturing of the parts, but protypes should be constructed and tested using a faster manufacturing technique.
